Equilima — Backtest

Key takeaways

  • Out-of-sample discipline: Tune on train, evaluate on holdout.
  • Indices as teachers: SPY/QQQ show regime shifts clearly.
  • Hypothetical: Past results may not repeat.

Snapshot — early April 2026, when hypothetical strategy posts flood social feeds—learn to verify

Walk-forward testing is how you simulate the experience of not knowing tomorrow. If you optimize a rule on all history including 2023–2025, you are implicitly cheating because you already saw those shocks. Instead, train on an earlier window, freeze parameters, and test forward—then roll the window. On SPY, you will see trend rules that shined in grinds and died in chop; on QQQ, tech-heavy drawdowns teach correlation shocks.

This week, ignore any ‘perfect’ backtest that does not show multiple walk segments—ask what happened in 2018, 2020, and 2022 separately.

Bull case — how this lens can help

You internalize that edge is rare and fragile—healthy skepticism.

Rolling tests reveal parameter drift before you commit capital.

Bear case — where it breaks

Walk-forward reduces but does not eliminate overfitting—you can still luck into segments.

Transaction costs still understate pain in live trading.

Bottom line

Run a two-window experiment in Equilima Backtest: tune on 2010–2018, test 2019–2024—journal where the rule broke.
