Stress cases that humble simple stories

Where disciplined investors get sloppy

Sell-side summaries are convenient and sometimes wrong on adjustments. When a headline metric on KO disagrees with the 10-Q, trust the filing. Non-GAAP add-backs deserve a skeptical highlight pass—especially stock comp, restructuring, and “adjusted” EBITDA lines that grow faster than GAAP operating income.

International revenue adds FX noise that screens ignore. If KO earns a large share overseas, a strong dollar can compress translated sales even when local demand is fine.
